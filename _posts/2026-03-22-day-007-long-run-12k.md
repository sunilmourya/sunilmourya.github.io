## What happened

Planned to run 10K. The route I took had a 2.4km extension I'd forgotten about — a loop around the reservoir that I'd mapped months ago and never cleaned up in Garmin. Discovered it at km 8 when the path forked and my watch was still confident.

Ran the extra 2.4. Not a mistake.

## The hypothesis I broke

I've been running easy (Z2) for two weeks, trying to build base. My hypothesis was that I should stay at 6:30/km to keep HR under 150.

Today at 6:02/km my HR held at 158. That's higher than I wanted but not as high as 6:02 "should" have been based on my Z2 estimates. Either my fitness improved faster than expected, or my zone calculations are off, or both.

I need to do a proper max HR test. The 220-minus-age formula is probably 8-10 bpm wrong for me. Getting the zones wrong means every "easy" run I've done has been at the wrong intensity.

**Action:** Do a field max HR test next Saturday. Then recalibrate zones.

## km 9 to 11

The part that's hard to write about: km 9 to 11 was genuinely hard. Not injured-hard, just tired-hard. Legs felt heavy. The internal negotiation started — *this is far enough, you planned 10 anyway*.

Ran through it. Finished.

I don't know how to write about this without sounding like a motivational poster. The only honest thing I can say is: the decision to continue happened somewhere below the level of conscious thought. The legs kept moving while the mind was still debating.

That's what I'm training. Not the lungs. The decision-making.

## Connection to coding

This is the third week I've noticed the pattern. Sunday long run → Monday coding clarity.

Something about sustained discomfort, especially the km 9-11 window, resets the anxiety dial. Problems that felt stuck on Friday feel approachable on Monday. I don't know the mechanism. I just know the correlation is consistent.
