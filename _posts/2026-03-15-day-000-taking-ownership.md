I almost didn't write this post.

Not because I don't have things to say — because writing it makes it real. And real things can fail publicly.

I'm writing it anyway.

---

## What crashed

I'm a senior systems developer. 12 years. C++, Rust, Android platform, automotive audio frameworks. On paper I look fine.

The honest version: I've been running on imposter syndrome for years. Delivering work but never quite believing I earned the room. Using AI tools to paper over gaps instead of filling them. Staying at a level below what I'm actually capable of because staying small feels safer than reaching and missing.

The career has drifted. A relationship is almost broken. Health has been neglected — I have diabetes that I've been managing instead of reversing. Weight I've been carrying instead of losing.

I'm done managing. I want to reverse it. All of it.

---

## Why Rust is the metaphor

There's a concept in Rust called ownership. Every value has exactly one owner. You can't pretend otherwise. The compiler will not let you avoid the question.

I've been avoiding ownership in my own life for a while — in my career choices, in my relationships, in my health decisions. Letting things drift. Blaming circumstances. Not taking the single-owner responsibility that actually changes things.

100 days of Rust is not just about Rust. It's practice for the other thing.

---

## The actual goals

**Rust & Backend**

Become a Rust monster. Not "comfortable with Rust." Not "familiar with Rust." The person who other developers ask.

The path: ownership and lifetimes from scratch → DSA without stdlib → build real backend systems with `axum` and `actix-web` → PostgreSQL with `sqlx` → ship something real.

No AI for core logic. 30-minute struggle minimum before any help. Plain editor for exercises. AI is for review, not generation. If AI wrote it, I rewrite it from memory the next day.

First milestone: remote Rust job. Not someday — this year.

**C++**

One deep topic per week. Move semantics, templates, concurrency. Connect everything to Rust — same problems, both languages. The C++ depth I already have is a moat. I'm going to use it.

**Running**

Started two days ago. 5km on March 14th. Legs remembered what to do.

Target: half-marathon. Under 2 hours. By Day 90.

Four runs per week. Log every single one, including the bad ones. Long run on Sundays.

Running is not separate from the rest of this. Running is where I practice the same thing — showing up when I don't feel like it, going further than feels comfortable, not quitting at km 9 just because I planned for km 8.

**Health**

Reverse the diabetes. Not manage — reverse.

Clean eating. Track it. No exceptions for "just this once" because "just this once" is how it got here.

Target weight: 73kg. That number is specific because vague targets don't work.

This is not about aesthetics. This is about living long enough to see what I'm capable of.

---

## The rules

These are not goals. Goals are things you hope for. Rules are things you follow.

- 30-minute struggle window before any AI help, timer on the desk
- Core Rust concepts (ownership, lifetimes, traits, async) — AI-free, full stop
- Write daily. Even 3 lines. Stuck is content. Bad days are content.
- Log every run.
- Log food. Every day.
- No exceptions without naming them out loud in this blog.

The public log is the accountability mechanism. If I skip a day, it shows. That's the point.

---

## What success looks like

Day 101. I can implement a doubly-linked list in Rust with correct lifetimes, from memory, in a plain editor, without help.

I have run 21km without stopping.

I have a remote Rust job, or am close enough to see it clearly.

I weigh less than I do today. The blood sugar numbers are moving in the right direction.

And I feel like I earned the room I'm standing in. Not because someone gave it to me. Because I built the floor myself.
