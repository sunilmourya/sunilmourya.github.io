Crash to Highs

I'm Sunil Mourya — a senior systems developer based in India with 12+ years building things that run close to the metal. C++ since before C++11 was even the standard. Android platform engineering at Samsung. Automotive audio frameworks. FFI, bindgen, the whole unglamorous stack.

The "crash" part is real. At some point the work stopped feeling like craftsmanship. The tools got easier, the thinking got shallower. AI assistants made it too easy to skip the struggle — and I noticed my muscle memory degrading.

When I built C++ depth, it was through days of banging my head against problems alone. Stack traces at 2am. That friction was the learning. I want Rust to feel the same.

The Rules

For the 100 days: minimum 30-minute struggle window before touching AI. Core Rust concepts (lifetimes, ownership, trait bounds, async) are AI-free zones. Plain editor for exercises. AI is for code review, not generation.

Why Running

Running taught me what coding sometimes forgets: consistency over heroics. Four slow runs beat one epic long run. And nothing clears a stuck algorithm like 7 kilometers of nothing but pavement and breathing.

What I'm Building Toward

Remote Rust contracting for international clients — SDV/automotive niche where my C++ background is actually an advantage. Longer term: productized tooling in the automotive Rust space. Audio debugging tools. FFI audit utilities. Things I'd have paid for at previous jobs.
